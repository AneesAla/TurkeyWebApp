Turkey is known for its rich cultural heritage and its stunning
landscapes. Despite its many strengths, the country faced a profound
challenge on February 6, 2023, when a severe earthquake brought
significant devastation and highlighted vulnerabilities in building
safety standards. Our mission is to raise awareness about the impacts of
this disaster and advocate for enhanced building regulations and safety
measures. By promoting a deeper understanding of these issues, we aim to
contribute to Turkey's resilience and ensure a safer future for its
communities.

Why Turkey is Important?


Culture

Turkey is culturally significant, holding a rich history that combines elements from various civilizations such as the Byzantine, Ottoman, and Hittite.


Religion

Turkey's historical religious landscape showcases its role as a crossroads of faiths. Iconic Istanbul structures like the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque, along with its title as the "land of thousands of mosques," highlight its deep Islamic heritage.

Tourism

Turkey's blend of Eastern and Western influences makes it a culturally appealing destination for a wide range of travelers, increasing its popularity as a tourism hotspot.

Culinary

Turkish cuisine features a rich mix of flavors, ingredients, and techniques, drawing from Ottoman, Middle Eastern, Central Asian, and Mediterranean culinary traditions.

History

Turkey's historical depth is marked by its role as a starting place for major civilizations. It has been home to the Hittites, Phrygians, Urartians, Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans, each enriching its diverse historical past.
